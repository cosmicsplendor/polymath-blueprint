OriginA throughline

Physics and mathematics were the original pull, since high school. Before software, before game dev, before security research, before any of this had a name. Fifteen years of software work, roughly ten behind and five ahead, is the accumulated technical range sitting on top of that pull. Security research is the most recent layer, and it trains something specific: finding what's true but unseen — the thing sitting in plain sight that nobody looked at closely enough to notice, because everyone agreed to stop questioning the abstraction around it.

The filing that follows isn't nine disciplines picked to see what sticks. It's the specific gaps: control, electrical, mechanical, aerospace - that currently sit outside that instinct's reach, closed deliberately so the same pattern-matching that finds hidden structure in code can find it in physical and hybrid systems too.

[0001]

Entry pointComputer graphics as the gateway discipline

Graphics is the rare subject that forces fluency in linear algebra, geometry, numerical methods, optimization, physics, parallel computing, and GPU architecture, all at once, all in service of something you can see render on screen.

A surprising amount of that fluency transfers directly into robotics, simulation, CAD, computer vision, and scientific computing. It's less a subject than a forcing function for everything else on this list.

MethodMathematics, revised

The goal isn't a 1,500-page pure math text. It's the mathematics engineers actually reach for, repeatedly, under pressure, while building things.

Linear algebra

Vectors, matrices, eigenvalues, SVD, coordinate transforms.

wherein this is probably the single most important subject on the list.

Calculus

Derivatives, partial derivatives, multiple integrals, vector calculus.

wherein the purpose is fluency sufficient to read physical models.

Differential equations

Mechanics, electronics, control, fluids, heat. everything eventually becomes a differential equation.

wherein this is the highest-leverage subject after linear algebra.

Probability

Underlies AI, estimation, robotics, finance, and reasoning under uncertainty generally.

Numerical methods

Newton's method, gradient descent, finite differences, finite elements, optimization.

wherein real engineers almost never solve equations exactly, they solve them approximately, and this is chronically underrated as a subject.

Deferred artQuantum mechanics — postponed

because almost every other discipline above raises invention capability sooner. Quantum becomes materially easier once linear algebra, probability, and differential equations are fluent.

At that point, Introduction to Quantum Mechanics (Griffiths) is the natural next step. If quantum computing specifically becomes interesting later, Quantum Computation and Quantum Information (Nielsen & Chuang) is the standard reference.

[0006]

Overlooked habitRead patents, a few hours a week

Because they teach four things nothing else does as directly:

how inventions are claimed what counts as novel thinking in embodiments & alternatives where the frontier actually is

Over a decade, hundreds of patents will likely sharpen engineering instinct as much as another textbook would.

Narrowing signalThe intersection nobody's standing in

Broad-by-necessity at the outset is correct — the resolution comes from doing. But one candidate thesis has already surfaced: blockchain engineering + game/graphics engineering, backed by a genuine physics foundation.

The overlap is close to empty. Blockchain engineers who understand consensus and cryptography rarely have the physics-and-rendering depth to build a simulation that's actually correct. Graphics engineers who can build a physically accurate simulation rarely understand proof systems or decentralized trust well enough to make that simulation verifiable to someone who didn't run it. The two skill trees live in different subcultures that don't read each other's work.

verifiable simulation on-chain physics engines provable procedural generation simulation-backed oracles digital-twin provenance

Common thread: any case where a simulation's output needs to be trusted by someone who didn't run it. It isn't a commitment — but a direction to keep checking.

[0008]

Review gate12-month checkpoint

The narrowing mechanism isn't more upfront planning — it's tracking which intersection generates unprompted "wait, what if..." moments during the work itself, versus which subjects were just consumed.

At month 12: review what actually produced ideas you couldn't stop thinking about, unprompted. Keep pursuing those. Let everything else remain scaffolding — background literacy that made the real thread legible.

The two-year plan is ambitious but plausible — if the objective is a deep engineering foundation rather than specialist mastery of every field on the list.

The software background is the real unlock here: decomposing complex systems, writing simulations, and reasoning algorithmically are already in hand. The two years can go almost entirely toward physical intuition, while software and AI serve as the tools that validate ideas faster.

[0007]

CheckpointReview gate — month twelve

The field list stays broad on purpose. Narrowing it now would be solving a discovery problem with a planning tool. The uncertainty resolves by building, not by adding more subjects to the filing.

At month twelve, the review isn't "did I learn enough." It's a single question: which intersection produced an idea I couldn't stop thinking about, unprompted? which combination of two things being learned simultaneously started generating "wait, what if…" on its own, without being chased.

That signal is what narrows nine fields into the decade-long invention track.
